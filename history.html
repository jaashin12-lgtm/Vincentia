History

Indigenous Culture

The Dharawal and Yuin peoples are the traditional custodians of this land and have strong ties to the Wandandian people. The Yuin people speak the Dhurga language, which is being actively preserved. (Microsoft - Search, 2022)

Cultural Experiences for Visitors

  • Traditional smoking ceremonies: “Gumaraa Welcome to Country” and “traditional Aboriginal dance troupe performances.”
  • Experiences: Tours from Gadhungal Marring, cultural workshops, and art workshops. Offers “history of the land, bush tucker, and plant medicine knowledge.”
  • Significant Places: “Gulaga (Mount Dromedary) and Biamanga National Park” hold creation stories of the Yuin people (Mather, K. 2024, June 12).

Colonial History

  • Colonial Beginnings: The towns original name was South Huskisson, Vincentia; founded in 1841 as a seaport built to transport wool from inland NSW to Jervis Bay.
  • Rebirth and Renaming: In 1952, developer Henry Halloran renamed the area Vincentia, after St Vincent County, honored John Jervis the 1st Earl of St Vincent named a a British navel hero
  • Today's Vincentia: Now part of the City of Shoalhaven, Vincentia; known for white sand beaches, Dhurga language education, and tourism that honors Indigenous knowledge and heritage.
