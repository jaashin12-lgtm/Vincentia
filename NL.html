Blenheim Beach

Small cove-like beach, renowned for its white sands, popular views, snorkeling place and exploring the rocks (clean amenities provided.)

Spend a whole day picnicing with the whole family

Barfleur Beach

A secluded beach surrounded by tall trees near a plant reserve, offering calm waters for swimming, reef banks and surfing.

Try a new skill like surfing for $50 per per person for 1.5 hours!

Greenfield Beach

Features white sand, crystal clear waters and a picnic area by the grass with BBQ and accessible facilities.

Take part in the take a picture 100 beaches challenge

Orion Beach

Known for its soft white sand and shallow waters, it is ideal for families, especially young kids and snorkeling.

Pack your sports equipment and play beach cricket, Frisbee, kick a ball, or hot potato with a volleyball!

Nelson Beach

Quiet area, perfect for swimming and walking, near plantation point, connecting to White Sands Walk. Its calm waters allow for paddle boarding opportunities as well.

Want to steer clear from the noise? Pack a mini sun lounge, a Copacabana and a good book to curl up on the beach!

Collingwood Beach

A 2.2 km beach, with accessible point to Elizabeth drive. Enjoy swimming, paddle boarding and exploring the rocks to discover new things about its history.

Hire a boat and to use the boat ramp for a watercraft adventure!

Moona Beach Park

Located near the Moona Moona Creek, featuring a lagoon popular with children (and less experienced swimmers) and picnics. The ideal spot for a cool down in summer months.

The clear waters a perfect for snorkeling for spotting small fish, then enjoy a self cooked BBQ lunch!

White Sands Beach/Walk

A scenic 2.5 km coastal trail from plantation point to Hyams beach, passing by several white sand beaches and bushlands.

Go for a sunrise or sunset walk to arrive at the beautiful white sands!

Hyams Beach

Famous for having some of the whitest sands in the world, a absolute must-visit for it beauty and swimming conditions.

Book a boating trip in the right seasons to see Dolphins and Whales!

Plantation Point Park

A reserve for picnics, playgrounds and access to Barfleur and Nelson beach. Also a great place for snorkeling and bioluminescent sights

Come in the evening to see the beautiful luminescent sight!

Stuart King Reserve

A coastal reserve near Nelson Beach, featuring native vegetation and walking paths. They aim to increase awareness of healthy vegetation against storm and for biodiversity.

Learn about the biodiversity and talk a nice morning walk with your coffee and drinks.

Booderee National Park

Managed by the Aboriginal community, it includes beaches, botanic gardens and historic sights like Cape St George Lighthouse.

Talk with the Aboriginal community and make personalized accessories from the land
